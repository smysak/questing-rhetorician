Rhetorician's Quest

You are a budding young rhetorician. Armed with the power of rhetoric, the world and all its glories lie before you. Will you take up the quest?

Behold, your way is stymied by a myriad of peasants, each involved in their own affairs. To achieve power, you must choose your words carefully, and bind them to your will.

Some peasants are succeptible to an emotional appeal. To convince them of your righteousness, you should choose words that emphasise care and concern.

Some peasants are succeptible to appeals which target their sense of credibility. To convince them of your virtue, you should choose words that emphasise your personal dynamism, or a sense of teamwork and unity.

Some peasants are only succeptible to logical appeals. To convince them of your integrity, you should choose words that do not skew meaning, nor seek to mislead.
